Purpose of the C&P Muscle Injuries Examination

Next

A C&P Muscle Injuries examination is different from a treatment examination in terms of the purpose of the examination and the audience for the examination report.

The Veterans Benefits Administration (VBA) requests a C&P examination when a Veteran or Servicemember files a claim for compensation or pension and the record does not contain sufficient information for an adjudicator to make a decision on the claim. The C&P Muscle Injuries examination is a legal, forensic examination requested by VBA to provide clinical information needed to assist VA in determining a claimant's entitlement to benefits. The audience for the documentation you provide from a C&P examination is primarily VA adjudicative staff, including Rating Veterans Service Representatives (RVSRs) and Decision Review Officers (DROs), and if the claim is appealed, attorneys and judges. The information requested from you, the examiner, is based solely on adjudication needs and not on treatment considerations.

By comparison, the purpose of an examination for treatment is to provide a diagnosis and determine appropriate treatment for a patient. The audience for a treatment examination report is primarily other clinicians.
