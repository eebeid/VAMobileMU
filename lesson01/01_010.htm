DeLuca Criteria

In the DeLuca v. Brown case, the U.S. Court of Appeals for Veterans Claims (CAVC) found that the method of assessing musculoskeletal disabilities that are rated based on limitation of motion was inadequate, as VA examinations did not adequately address additional limitation of function of joints that might occur due to weakness, excess fatigability, or incoordination, or to pain on use or during flare-ups. As a result of the DeLuca case, these additional factors must be considered for each joint examined to ensure that functional impairment is not underestimated:

  1. Pain on movement that limits function during repetitive use and flare-ups
  2. Weakened movement
  3. Excess fatigability
  4. Incoordination

You should assess for each of these and report in the number of degrees, if feasible, additional loss of motion of the joint as a result of one or more of these factors. The absence of any or all of these factors should also be noted.

Example: After using a roller to paint for 20 minutes, the Veteran is unable to raise his arm above shoulder level due to pain and fatigue.
