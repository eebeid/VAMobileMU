Legal Aspects of the C&P Muscle Injuries Examination

Next

As you know, C&P examinations serve a legal purpose. This topic will include both muscle injuries and musculoskeletal injuries, with guidance from title 38, part 4, of the Code of Federal Regulations, VA's Schedule for Rating Disabilities.

For the purposes of this course, the cardinal signs and symptoms of muscle disability are loss of power, weakness, lowered threshold of fatigue, fatigue-pain, impairment of coordination, and uncertainty of movement (38 CFR 4.56).

If on examination you discern that a muscle injury affects the functioning of a joint, you will need to address the affected joint with the appropriate documentation protocol. Please note that some regulations have acquired broader applications from court decisions, such as 38 CFR 4.59. This regulation discusses considerations for arthritis that have been interpreted by the courts to apply to musculoskeletal injuries.
