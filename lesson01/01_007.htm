Loss of Use

Next

In cases of severe muscle disabilities affecting the hand, foot, or buttocks that rise to the level of loss of use, the claimant may be entitled to additional compensation. This additional compensation, known as special monthly compensation1 (SMC), is an additional monthly monetary payment, over and above any disability compensation that may otherwise be paid. One of the relevant regulations, 38 CFR 4.63, for example, defines loss of use of a hand or foot, for purposes of special monthly compensation, as existing when there is no more effective function than would be possible if there were to be an amputation at the site of election below elbow or knee with a prosthesis in place. This by no means implies that an amputation needs to be indicated or recommended in order for loss of use to be present. It means that there is very limited remaining function of a hand, based on grasping, manipulation, etc., or of a foot, based on balance, propulsion, etc., to the extent that function would be comparable to, or at least no better than, the function that could be obtained by means of a prosthesis.

Another regulation, 38 CFR 4.64, defines loss of use of both buttocks, involving severe damage to muscle Group XVII bilaterally.

 

1VBA uses 38 CFR 3.350 (Special monthly compensation ratings) for guidance.
