You play an important role in the C&P process when you conduct a Muscle Injuries examination. It is your responsibility as a C&P examiner to gather information needed for adjudicating a Veteran's or Servicemember's claim. This lesson covers the purpose and scope of the C&P Muscle Injuries examination. Legal aspects of this examination, including those stemming from the Code of Federal Regulations (CFR) and case law, in particular, the DeLuca v. Brown (1995) and the Mitchell v. Shinseki (2011) cases, are also covered.

When you complete this lesson, you should be able to recognize the purpose of conducting a C&P Muscle Injuries examination for adjudication purposes.
