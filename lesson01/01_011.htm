Mitchell Criteria

The Mitchell v. Shinseki (2011) case expanded upon the evaluation criteria outlined by the CAVC in DeLuca (1995) by heightening VA's duty to consider limitation of joint function due to flare-ups or with repetitive use.

For a C&P examination, a flare-up is generally regarded as any temporary or recurring significant increase in signs or symptoms associated with the condition. If there are flare-ups of joint disease that affect limitation of motion of a joint, you should report the duration, frequency, and severity of the flare-ups as described by the claimant. Based on the Mitchell v. Shinseki decision, you also need to provide the following information:

  1. Describe whether pain, weakness, fatigability, or incoordination could significantly limit functional ability during flare-ups, or when the joint is used repeatedly over a period of time.
  2. Describe any such additional limitation due to pain, weakness, fatigability, or incoordination. If feasible, this opinion should be expressed in terms of the degrees of additional range of motion loss due to pain on use or during flare-up.
  3. If such opinion is not feasible, please state so and provide an explanation as to why the opinion regarding additional limitation during flare-up or when the joint is used repeatedly over a period of time cannot be rendered.

Note that for each examination you need to provide this information for both flare-ups and when the affected joint is used repeatedly over a period of time, not just after three repetitions.

Example: After three hours in a photo session, Staff Sergeant Marcus is unable to raise a camera to a 90-degree angle above his shoulder due to increased pain and fatigue.
