It's vital that you conduct and document a thorough and comprehensive examination to avoid having your report deemed insufficient or returned due to a need for clarification. Returned examination reports delay the process and clog up the Compensation and Pension pipeline. It is important to complete your examination report right the first time. With this in mind, apply these five rules:

  1. Complete all necessary Disability Benefits Questionnaires (DBQs) or other documentation protocols.
  2. Address all questions listed on the Examination Request.
  3. Give an opinion with a comprehensive rationale when requested.
  4. Consider and address administered test results.
  5. Substantiate all diagnoses.
