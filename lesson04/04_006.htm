Discrepancies in Diagnosis

You may find conditions in your examination that differ from or contradict previous diagnoses in the Veteran's or Servicemember's records. If this situation arises, document the new diagnoses and provide a well-reasoned rationale for the change in diagnosis, including any pertinent examination or test results. It's also helpful to use available lay statements from the Veteran or Servicemember to document a condition.

Discrepancies in Reported Symptoms

If you feel that reported symptoms are not supported by the physical examination, test results, or observations, include a statement similar to this one in your examination report: "No objective findings to support a diagnosis of the claimed muscle condition." "Normal" is a valid diagnosis. Along the same lines, if the findings do not support the level of severity suggested by the complaints, explain in detail why. Record the Veteran's or Servicemember's own unprompted language as his or her statements and not your conclusions. Remember, however, the Veteran or Servicemember is qualified to describe his or her muscle symptoms, as he or she is able to see and feel the condition.
