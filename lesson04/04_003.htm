Completing your documentation as soon as possible after an examination will help keep your report accurate. Your documentation is not complete until you have addressed all claimed conditions by providing your examination findings and pertinent test results. Include the chronological history of treatment. Once a diagnosis has been confirmed, describe current signs, symptoms, and physical findings, especially functional limitations or impairments, related to the muscle conditions.

You'll also want to refer to the Examination Request as you write your final report. This practice will help you meet adjudication requirements. Before signing off on your report, check to ensure you addressed all instructions or questions posed in the Remarks section of the Examination Request. Remember to always document whether or not the C-file was forwarded to you and reviewed by you.
