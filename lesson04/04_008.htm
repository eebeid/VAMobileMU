When to Provide a Medical Opinion

No opinion was required for Staff Sergeant Marcus's examination, but there are important considerations for medical opinions when you are asked to provide one. In most cases, an opinion should be given only when the Examination Request requires it or if the evidence of record necessitates providing an opinion. For example, the evidence of record might require a medical opinion to explain findings such as a diagnosis that differs from previous diagnoses.

Follow the Examination Request

If an opinion is requested on an Examination Request, review the Examination Request carefully to ensure that all required information is supplied and your opinion addresses only the information needed. Avoid expressing an opinion regarding the legal merits of any claim or the percentage evaluation that should be assigned for a disability. Adjudicators will make those determinations based on your examination findings and other evidence.

An Adequate Opinion

An adequate opinion for C&P purposes is a responsive conclusion supported by a thoughtful, logical, robust rationale based on medical literature, when feasible, and all available evidence. It should address how and if a Veteran's or Servicemember's medical issues are related to his or her military service or respond to other specific questions posed on the Examination Request.
