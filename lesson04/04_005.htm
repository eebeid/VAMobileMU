Diagnosis Do and Do Not
Do Do Not
Do provide a diagnosis for every condition listed on the examination request. Do not ignore any conditions listed on the examination request.
Do provide a specific diagnosis instead of signs and symptoms, for example, hypertension instead of elevated blood pressure and arthritis instead of arthralgia. Do not provide a differential diagnosis or use phrases such as "rule out."
Do interpret laboratory findings and explain how they relate to diagnoses. Do not say that additional studies, evaluations, or laboratory tests are necessary for a final diagnosis. Ensure additional assessments are completed and include the results as part of the examination report.
Do substantiate each diagnosis. Do not add comments that undermine a diagnosis, e.g., "While the veteran may have post-concussive headaches, some of his symptoms may be due to atypical seizures, and an EEG would be helpful."
Do provide an explanation if no diagnosis is possible based on the evidence of record and examination findings. Do not simply state that no diagnosis is possible.
