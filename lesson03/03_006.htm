Now that the medical history interview is finished, the examiner focuses on considerations for the physical examination. The examiner has already verified that a measuring tape and goniometer are available in the examination room.

The examiner knows that the injuries requiring C&P Muscle Injuries examinations are difficult to evaluate due to possible overlapping damage to muscle, tendons, nerves, and bones. Disabilities resulting from these wounds can result in functional loss affecting range of motion, strength, speed, coordination, and endurance. These disabilities may be caused by anatomical damage, pain, adhesions, defective innervation, or other pathology.

The examiner is aware that some muscle conditions may be painful, so he will alert Staff Sergeant Marcus before he touches him. The examiner will assess and grade muscle strength using a scale similar to the one on the next page.
