A review of relevant records may include a claims file (C-file) and any available medical records from electronic sources, including Virtual VA (VVA) or the Veterans Benefits Management System (VBMS).

The C-file

For C&P examinations, you may be asked to review the claimant's C-file in paper or electronic form, or both. When a C-file is required for review, VBA will generally state this on the Examination Request. In turn, you must review the entire C-file and document that you have done so in your examination report. A C-file may contain the following information:

  • History of the claimed condition
  • Service personnel records, including Department of Defense separation form (DD 214) or equivalent
  • Entrance history and physical examination report
  • Treatments during military service
  • Exit history and physical examination report
  • Private medical information the claimant has submitted to the RO or authorized VA to obtain on his or her behalf
If you will be conducting an increase or review examination, carefully review the last C&P examination of record, as you will be focusing on a medical history from the last C&P examination to the current one.
