Background


Born on June 18, 1963, Simon Marcus served in the U.S. Army from June 1981 to June 1985, and then served as an Army reservist. He was called back to active duty a few times, for Desert Storm February 1991–March 1992, Iraq 2007–2008 and Afghanistan 2011–2012. In Afghanistan he was injured on March 11, 2012 during a mortar attack, resulting in five puncture wounds and embedded shrapnel in the upper right arm. He was treated in Afghanistan, then evacuated to Germany, and finally was sent to the U.S. where he was hospitalized through October 2012.
