Congratulations! You have completed the DMA Muscle Injuries Examination course. You may use the course Menu to navigate to any lesson as needed before you proceed to the Final Assessment that follows this summary. In four lessons, this course provided the following content.

The C&P Muscle Injuries Examination

This lesson discussed the scope and purpose of the C&P Muscle Injuries examination and the differences between an examination for treatment and a C&P examination for legal purposes. The lesson also explained your role as a C&P examiner. Legal aspects of the examination, including reporting requirements, loss of use from the Code of Federal Regulations (CFR), and DeLuca and Mitchell criteria were also covered.

Before the Examination

This lesson described preparation for a C&P Muscle Injuries examination. The focus was on the review of documents that may include the Examination Request, the C-file, all available medical records, and the documentation protocol required for the examination. Direct any questions regarding these documents to the regional office (RO) or Appeals Management Center (AMC) before the examination, if possible. The case study was supported by an Examination Request and a summary of details gathered from the C-file by the examiner.
