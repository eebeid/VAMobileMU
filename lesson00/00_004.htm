Terminal Learning Objective

At the completion of this course, you should be able to identify the criteria and recognize the general process to prepare for, conduct, and document a C&P Muscle Injuries examination. This includes the use of an appropriate documentation protocol to assess the presence and extent of disability according to VA regulations, policies, directives, guidelines, and DMA requirements.

Enabling Learning Objectives

Mastery of the following four enabling learning objectives will help you meet the terminal learning objective:

  1. Recognize the purpose of conducting a C&P Muscle Injuries examination for adjudication purposes versus an examination for treatment, given an overview of the C&P Muscle Injuries examination.
  2. Identify preparation requirements for a C&P Muscle Injuries examination, given an overview of the process to prepare for a C&P Muscle Injuries examination.
  3. Identify requirements and process for conducting a C&P Muscle Injuries examination, given direction on how to perform a C&P Muscle Injuries examination.
  4. Identify documentation requirements of a C&P Muscle Injuries examination, given an overview of requirements.

The standards for this course are found in relevant sections of the United States Code (U.S.C.), the Code of Federal Regulations (CFR), Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) directives, manuals from VHA and the Veterans Benefits Administration (VBA), and in guidance from DMA.
